State v. U. Ghani & Others

High-Profile Trial under Section 322 PPC (Qatal Bil Sabab)

Case Overview

State v. U. Ghani & Others was an extremely sensitive and high-profile Sessions trial in which the accused faced charges under Section 322 of the Pakistan Penal Code (Qatal Bil Sabab). The case involved the brutal killing of a young girl, with the driver allegedly involved in transporting her also killed. The accused were alleged to have had an illicit liaison with the victim. The case generated immense media and public attention due to the violent nature of the crime and the sensational allegations.

Prosecution Case

The prosecution relied on a combination of circumstantial evidence and forensic pointers:

  • 01

    Vaginal Retention of Sperm: Cited as medical evidence to link the accused to the victim.

  • 02

    CCTV Footage: Surveillance recordings attempting to trace the movements of the victim and the accused.

  • 03

    CDR Evidence: Call detail records to establish contact between the accused and the victim.

  • 04

    Eyewitness Statements: Statements regarding the presence of accused with the victim on the date in question.

Defence Strategy

The defence undertook a methodical approach to dismantle the prosecution's circumstantial and forensic claims:

Vaginal Sperm Evidence

Challenged the conclusiveness of the medical evidence and emphasized the limitations in establishing temporal correlation.

CCTV and CDR Evidence

Examined issues of authenticity, chain of custody, and legal admissibility under Pakistani law.

Circumstantial Evidence

Highlighted inconsistencies, improbabilities, and gaps that prevented establishing guilt beyond reasonable doubt.

Benefit of Doubt Doctrine

Argued that every lacuna in circumstantial and forensic evidence must be interpreted in favour of the accused.

Judicial Findings

The Hon'ble Chief Court, after careful scrutiny of the evidence, concluded that:

    Circumstantial evidence was insufficient to establish the accused's guilt beyond reasonable doubt.

  • âœ¦

Result: The accused were acquitted of all charges.

Legal Significance

This case underscores several critical principles:

  • 01

    Circumstantial evidence must satisfy strict judicial scrutiny and be complete and conclusive.

  • 02

    Forensic evidence must be corroborated by direct evidence or other admissible material.

  • 03

    Benefit of doubt remains a cornerstone of criminal trials.

  • 04

    Admissibility of modern electronic evidence (CCTV, CDR) is subject to legal verification.
